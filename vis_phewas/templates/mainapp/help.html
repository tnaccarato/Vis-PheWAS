Node Appearance and Meaning

Nodes represent different categories, diseases, or alleles and are visually distinct based on various factors:

  • Color: Disease nodes are colored on a yellow-orange gradient based on the number of associated alleles. Allele nodes are colored according to whether they are protective (blue) or risk factors (red).
  • Node Size: The size of allele nodes indicates the statistical significance (p-value) of the association. Larger nodes represent more significant results.
  • Border Thickness: The thickness of an allele node's border indicates the magnitude of the odds ratio, with thicker borders showing a greater deviation from 1.
  • Shared Alleles: If an allele is associated with multiple diseases, lines will connect the relevant nodes, displaying links between the diseases.

Allele Details

Click on an allele node to display detailed information in the sidebar.

The sidebar will show the odds ratio, p-value, and confidence bounds for the allele in the selected disease. You can also view all other diseases associated with the allele and the most protected and most affected diseases.

Clicking on a disease in the sidebar will add it to the filter panel, allowing you to refine your exploration further.

Filters

Use the "Add Filter" button at the bottom of the screen to apply filters to the visualized data. Filters can be applied to allele type, disease category, p-value, odds ratio, and more.

Select the field to filter by and choose from options like "contains," "exactly," "greater than," "less than," etc., depending on the data type.

You can apply up to 8 filters and combine them using AND or OR logical operators for more precise queries.

Once filters are applied, a notification will appear showing the applied filters, and the network graph will update to display only relevant nodes.

Exporting Data

After applying filters, you can export the filtered data to a CSV file for further analysis by clicking the "Export" button. The CSV file will include the filtered dataset along with a summary of the filters used.

Self-Organising Map (SOM) Clustering

Use the "Tools" tab on the left sidebar to generate a Self-Organising Map (SOM) visualisation. You can choose whether to cluster diseases or alleles.

You can further refine the clustering by selecting specific disease categories and number of clusters. The updated SOM will display as a heatmap, showing clusters with their relationships.

The clustered data can also be exported as a CSV file for further analysis.

Additional Options

In the "Options" tab, you can filter HLA alleles by 2- or 4-digit resolution to display serotypes or subtypes.
